High-intensity interval training (HIIT) does not directly increase height, as final height is determined mainly by genetics and the closure of growth plates, which typically occurs in the late teens or early twenties. However, HIIT can indirectly support growth and development by increasing growth hormone (GH) secretion and promoting overall health. Exercises like pull-ups are beneficial for spinal health, which may promote length and flexibility. How HIIT may support growth Growth Hormone (GH) Release: HIIT has been shown to increase the secretion of growth hormone, especially after a single bout of exercise. More research is needed to determine if regular HIIT significantly increases long-term GH levels and contributes to daily GH secretion. Overall Health: HIIT promotes cardiovascular health, muscle strength, and bone density, which are important for reaching your full genetic potential for height. Efficient for Children: HIIT can be an efficient and enjoyable way for children to be physically active, which is crucial for their development. Exercises to include in a HIIT routine Pull-ups: These are excellent for strengthening the back and can help decompress the spine, promoting flexibility and length. Jump Squats: This explosive exercise can build lower body strength and power. Mountain Climbers: A great core exercise that elevates your heart rate. Lunge Jumps: This move combines strength and power, targeting your legs and glutes. Important considerations Age: Height growth is largely genetically determined and stops after the growth plates fuse, typically around ages 18-20. Safety: HIIT is intense and should be approached with caution. It is not recommended for individuals with certain pre-existing conditions, such as heart issues. A doctor should be consulted before starting a new exercise program. Exercise is not a substitute for genetics: While exercise is essential for overall health and reaching your natural potential, it cannot change your genetic blueprint for height.
